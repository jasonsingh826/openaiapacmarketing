The Strategy · OpenAI · Asia-Pacific

APAC isn't a market. It's twelve markets at twelve stages of AI maturity.

A world-class regional partner program doesn't run one playbook. It holds the framework constant and flexes execution across three market archetypes — varying partner mix, persona enablement, and priorities by market. This is how I'd build the best partner marketing program at OpenAI, anywhere in the region.

01 · The regional thesis

One framework, executed three ways.

The framework on the previous page is deliberately company-agnostic and region-agnostic — partner taxonomy, the three-stage journey, persona enablement, measurement. That's the constant. What changes across APAC is execution, and it changes along four dimensions at once. The art of the regional role is flexing all four together without losing the consistency that makes the program scalable.

Dimension 1

Market archetype

Maturity, IT spend, regulation, and AI adoption cluster markets into three groups — each gets a different default motion.

Dimension 2

Partner-type mix

The weighting of agencies, SIs, resellers, tech partners, and ISVs shifts dramatically by market.

Dimension 3

Persona localization

The four-persona enablement matrix localizes — language, proof, format, and in-market certification.

Dimension 4

Priority play

Each market has a single highest-leverage move — the one play that earns the right to everything else.

02 · Market archetypes

Three archetypes that decide how we show up.

Treating fifteen countries as fifteen plans is unmanageable; treating them as one is naïve. Clustering into three archetypes is how the program stays both scalable and locally credible.

Archetype A · high-touch

Anchor Markets

Japan · Korea · Australia/NZ · Singapore

Mature enterprise IT, established GSI and strong local-SI ecosystems, regulated industries, high baseline AI adoption. Singapore is the regional anchor — OpenAI's APAC hub, the S$300M+ "OpenAI for Singapore" commitment, the first Applied AI Lab outside the US, and roughly one in four residents on ChatGPT.

  • Partners: GSIs + major local SIers
  • Motion: 1:1 co-marketing, joint ABM, exec moments
  • Priority: regulated enterprise transformation
Archetype B · blended

Scale Market — India

A category of its own

The GCC capital of the world, home to IT-services majors that deliver globally, an enormous developer population, and one of the densest startup ecosystems anywhere. India partners are uniquely high-leverage — they serve the India market and carry OpenAI into enterprises worldwide.

  • Partners: IT-services majors, GCCs, developers, startups
  • Motion: blended — 1:1 with majors, 1:many for the builder long tail
  • Priority: developer scale + IT-services-led enterprise
Archetype C · scaled

Velocity Markets

Indonesia · Vietnam · Thailand · Philippines · Malaysia

Mobile-first, digital-native, fast-growing, and price-sensitive, with local language essential to adoption. The ecosystem runs on regional SIs, resellers, telcos, digital agencies, and local startups rather than global GSIs.

  • Partners: regional SIs, resellers, telcos, digital agencies
  • Motion: 1:many — campaign-in-a-box, reseller coverage
  • Priority: scaled adoption + digital-native builders
Scope · Greater China

OpenAI does not offer services in mainland China or Hong Kong, so they sit outside direct GTM scope. The region engages Chinese-headquartered multinationals through their operations in in-scope markets where relevant, and monitors Taiwan separately. Naming this keeps the plan honest and the investment focused where OpenAI actually sells.

04 · Persona enablement, localized

Four personas — now in seven languages and twelve markets.

The persona matrix from the framework is the engine; localization is the fuel. The benchmark to beat is the best in the category: role-based learning paths that reach baseline competency in weeks, delivered free, rolled out in regional languages. In Japan and Korea especially, where the language barrier is real, localized enablement isn't a nicety — it's the entire reason a partner can sell at all.

Technical / Engineering

Build in-language, locally

  • Reference architectures & build labs localized for in-market stacks
  • Documentation in Japanese, Korean, Bahasa, Thai, Vietnamese
  • Local-timezone office hours and deployment support
Sales

Local proof beats global proof

  • In-market case studies and ROI tooling, not US logos
  • In-language sales plays and demo scripts
  • Local competitive context — open-weight and regional model providers
Marketing

Campaign-in-a-box, localized

  • In-language co-marketing kits and brand guidelines
  • Channel-appropriate formats per market (e.g. messaging apps in SEA)
  • Local event and webinar templates
Programs / Alliance

Run the plan in-market

  • Regional joint-business-planning templates and cadence
  • Localized MDF process and approvals
  • In-language certification delivery that gates tiering and benefits
Sequencing

English-first to launch fast, then a prioritized regional-language rollout led by the anchor and velocity markets where language is the binding constraint. Certification is localized last-mile so the credential — and the benefits it unlocks — mean the same thing in every market.

06 · The localization operating model

The dual mandate: localize what's central, build what's regional.

A regional program fails in one of two ways — by reinventing what global already built, or by shipping global assets that don't land locally. The operating model draws a clear line between what's inherited, what's adapted, and what's created in-market.

Central provides

Global OpenAI

  • → Master brand & narrative
  • → Product launches & roadmap
  • → Partner program architecture — tiers & certification curriculum
  • → MDF policy & measurement architecture
  • → Flagship global alliances
Region adapts

APAC

  • → The three archetype playbooks
  • → APAC tentpole moments & calendar
  • → Regional partner tiering priorities
  • → Localized enablement tracks & campaigns
  • → APAC's voice in global planning
Local creates

In-market

  • → Language localization
  • → Local customer proof & references
  • → Market-specific events
  • → Local partner recruitment
  • → In-language certification delivery
07 · The three goals

Everything ladders up to three goals.

Goal 01

Convert OpenAI's flagship alliances into APAC pipeline

Take the global alliances and platform partnerships and build archetype-specific joint plans: 1:1 co-marketing and joint ABM in the anchor markets, IT-services-led motions in India, scaled reseller programs in the velocity markets. The goal is partner-sourced and partner-influenced pipeline that wouldn't exist otherwise.

Demand creation + capture
Goal 02

Activate the developer & startup ecosystem through partners

Partner-led education, certification, hackathons, builder communities, and startup programs — concentrated where the builders are: India, Southeast Asia, and Singapore. Earn the trust of the people who decide what gets built on, and feed bottoms-up adoption that pulls enterprise demand behind it.

Demand creation
Goal 03

Build the APAC partner marketing operating system

The archetype playbooks, the localization model, the planning rhythm, the certification localization, and the measurement architecture — the machine that makes goals 1 and 2 repeatable. Plus representing APAC's reality in global planning so the region gets the assets and investment it needs.

Foundation
08 · Borrowed from the best

Five mechanics the category leaders prove out — adapted for OpenAI in APAC.

The best partner programs in the world already ran the experiments. The plan borrows what works and skips what doesn't.

From AWS

Certification unlocks money

Competencies that gate funding, visibility, and co-sell — because validated expertise is a top buyer-selection criterion. Certification is a benefit engine, not a checkbox.

From Google Cloud

Reward outcomes, kill admin

Recognize pre-sales influence and post-sales success over paperwork, and automate tier tracking — so partners spend time with customers, not on forms.

From Snowflake

Role-based, fast, localized

Persona learning tracks that reach competency in weeks, free, with regional-language rollout — the model for localized enablement at APAC scale.

From Databricks

Scale the trained base

Treat enablement as a growth engine — a relentlessly scaled, role-based learning machine that can multiply the certified partner base inside a year.

From Anthropic

Open door, structured start

A low-friction front door paired with a structured onboarding and a success manager — so a partner gets from "signed" to "selling" without stalling.

The synthesis

Every leader runs the same spine: open entry, role-based certification that gates real benefits, tiered co-investment, a co-sell engine, and outcome-based measurement. The APAC edge is executing that spine localized, by archetype — faster and more in-market than anyone else.
