§ 05 · The Bets

Two build the brand. One
closes. One scales.

Disproportionate energy goes here. The bets are the heroes; the engines and foundation execute them.

A
Demand creation

Customer Advocacy Engine

The primary demand creation engine in a peer-led, trust-gated market. Not a content program — the core trust-building machine.

  • Customers talking about adoption in many formats — narratives, speakers, data points, peer forums, not just case studies
  • Multiple local, in-vertical proofs per market — buyers trust peers, not vendors or analysts
  • Customer speakers at every event, summit, and roundtable
  • "Call my peer at [company]" as the final trust unlock — dark marketing, operationalized
B
Demand creation

Enterprise Brand & Thought Leadership

An enterprise brand identity distinct from the consumer brand — because consumer awareness doesn't transfer into enterprise trust.

  • Enterprise promise: "the most trusted AI platform to build your business on" — different audience, proof, register
  • APAC voice: trust-forward, measured, outcome-oriented; calibrated by market (JP/KR formal, India scale, ANZ direct, SEA momentum)
  • Executive voice program; trust as a permanent brand pillar, not a campaign
  • Thought leadership circuit + hospitality for key accounts
C
Demand capture

Account-Based Everything + Vertical Summits

The sales-embedded pipeline conversion engine. Committee-mapped. Turns brand into logos.

  • Tight SDR/BDM alignment; committee-mapped invitations to every activation
  • Vertical summit series — FSI and public sector first; customer speakers, partner solutions, exec access
  • PLG → enterprise: arm internal Builder champions to make the business case upward
  • Always-on nurture engineered to committee and awareness stage
D
Creation + capture

Partner Marketing

The engine that gives OpenAI coverage, trust, and value delivery across APAC. Recruit → Enable → Scale.

  • Recruit — targeted digital and physical activations; seamless to join, right types, right markets
  • Enable — sales, technical, marketing, and programs enablement across all four partner audiences
  • Scale — MDF, dedicated resources, joint campaigns with top 5–10 partners
  • Three focus areas: enablement, joint activation, frictionless recruitment

ScaleMDF, joint campaigns, co-sell, partners woven into company-led campaigns

As models commoditize, partners have choice. They won't pick OpenAI for a benchmark score — they'll pick us because we make it easy to join, easy to build, and profitable to grow. In APAC, partners aren't a nice-to-have: they're the coverage model, the trust layer, and the value-realization engine.

The partner ecosystem — five types, five roles

Agencies

Creative, media, digital — build on the platform for clients; extend reach and creative credibility.
Need: co-marketable proof, campaign-ready assets

SIs & Consulting

GSIs and consultancies that architect, deliver, and manage deployments; carry trust, open doors.
Need: enablement, joint solutions, exec air-cover

Resellers

Package and transact, especially mid-market and regional; provide market coverage.
Need: margin clarity, sales plays, lead flow

Technology partners

Platforms whose products integrate with OpenAI to create joint value; extend the product surface.
Need: joint narratives, co-sell motions

ISVs & builders

Software companies and startups building products on the platform; drive innovation and breadth.
Need: technical enablement, launch support
The partner maturity cycle
1Recruit
Get the right partners interested and make starting effortless. Not volume — the right types in the right markets, activated fast.
  • Targeted recruitment campaigns — digital and physical
  • A published partner value proposition by type
  • Frictionless enrolment — self-serve, fast agreements
  • Guided 90-day activation with milestone triggers
  • Recruitment through proof — partners recruiting partners
Measured: new partners by type/market · time-to-first-value · % activated in 90 days
2Enable
Make partners independently able to build, sell, and market — across every audience inside the partner. The single biggest lever, and the stage most programs do badly.
  • Content backbone — sales plays, dev docs, campaign kits, comms
  • Partner communications on a reliable operating cadence
  • Events — workshops, trainings, certification bootcamps
  • Certification by audience — gates MDF, co-sell, tier, visibility
Measured: certification coverage by audience · content utilization · engagement score
3Scale
Invest in enabled partners with dollars, people, and programs. Two gears: programmatic scale for the base, high-touch co-investment for the top 5–10.
  • 1:many — MDF marketplace, campaign-in-a-box, syndicated content, partner-led events
  • 1:1 — joint demand campaigns, joint events, dedicated resources, joint ABM, customer proof
  • Partner-led expansion — value realization that feeds the advocacy flywheel
Measured: partner-sourced & influenced pipeline · MDF ROI · attach rate

§ 08 · Measurement Framework

Every number connects
to an action.

Three tiers. Campaign metrics roll up into KPIs; KPIs drive topline goals. No vanity metrics.

Tier 1 · Topline goals
Goal 01 · Brand

Brand sentiment in APAC

Are we becoming the trusted, preferred platform in the minds of enterprise buyers? Brand tracking, share of voice, unaided awareness, and trust/security perception — market by market. The leading indicator that Create is working.

Goal 02 · Accounts

Net-new accounts acquired

Are we converting enterprise buyers into customers? The commercial bottom line. Acquisition first; expansion is phase-2.

Tier 2 · Operational KPIs
Create
Brand & demand creation
  • Share of voice vs. competitors, by market
  • Thought leadership reach & engagement
  • Referenceable customers by market/vertical
  • Hospitality: accounts engaged, progression
  • Community: attendance, follow-on signals
Capture
Pipeline & conversion
  • Marketing-sourced pipeline created
  • Converted to opportunity
  • Closed to revenue
  • Target accounts engaged (ABE coverage)
  • Summits: accounts attending, pipeline/event
  • PLG conversion; nurture stage progression
Partner
Ecosystem health
  • Partners recruited by type/market
  • Partner engagement score
  • Content utilization & certification coverage
  • MDF utilization & MDF pipeline
  • Partner-sourced & influenced pipeline
  • Partner attach rate on deals

Expansion KPIs (phase-2, parked): account growth & expansion revenue · usage-based expansion · partner-led value realization scores.

Tier 3 · Campaign metrics

Events & activations

Registrations, attendance rate, target account & persona coverage, post-event meetings & pipeline, NPS.

Content & thought leadership

Views, downloads, shares by asset; engagement by format/channel; content-attributed leads & pipeline.

Digital & campaigns

Impressions, reach, CTR, cost per lead/MQL/opp, stage conversion, channel performance.

ABE / account-based

Account coverage, committee coverage, account progression, account-level pipeline velocity.

Partner campaigns

Partner-led reach & engagement, partner-channel leads, co-marketed pipeline, MDF campaign ROI.

Nurture

Pool size & growth, stage progression rate, nurture-to-opportunity conversion, scoring accuracy.

Operating rhythm
Weekly

Campaign metrics reviewed by program owners — what's working, what to adjust.

Monthly

KPI review with marketing leadership — are indicators healthy, where to intervene.

Quarterly

Topline goals to GM & leadership — sentiment, pipeline, partner health.

Annually

Full strategy review — are the goals right, is allocation working, what shifts.

Campaign metrics → KPIs → Topline goals

If brand sentiment is flat, look at Create KPIs — is advocacy producing, is thought leadership landing? If pipeline is short, look at Capture KPIs — is ABE covering enough accounts, are summits converting, are partners sourcing? If partner attach is low, look at partner KPIs — is enablement landing, is MDF utilized, are the right partners certified? Every number connects to an action.

§ 09 · Phase-2Account expansion
  • Acquisition first. Expansion activates after the land motion is running — parked, not forgotten.

  • Outcome advocacy — feeding back into Bet A, the customer advocacy engine
