The core argument

There is no AI leader in APAC yet. Four moves decide whether OpenAI becomes it.

01
Make markets, move first. No vendor owns APAC and the field is genuinely open — competitors haven't formally landed at scale and open-weight is fragmented. That window won't stay open: vendors will start to lock up countries, verticals, personas, and partners. Land priority markets first — making markets beats fighting uphill as a late entrant.
02
Lead with trust. APAC buyers are more sovereignty-conscious and vendor-skeptical, and recent trust concerns will lengthen cycles. Trust, security, and sovereignty become the foundational narrative — and the work has to start now, not when the deal is on the table.
03
Win on local peer proof. Buyers no longer trust vendors or analysts — they trust their circle of peers. APAC needs many local, in-vertical, in-country proof points: narratives, speakers, data, advocacy. Peer proof sits at the center of the plan, not the periphery.
04
Sell outcomes, deliver value through partners. Enterprises are shifting from adoption to value — sharper still in price-sensitive APAC. Outcomes are the proof buyers need before they buy, and a strong partner ecosystem is how that value gets delivered at scale.
