§ 01 · Considerations for APAC GTM

Five truths that change
the whole game.

APAC is not a discount version of the US playbook. Five forces reshape how buyers decide — and together they imply four strategic imperatives everything else ladders to.

01
Competitive pressure is a headwind
Anthropic is emerging as one credible enterprise contender, but no one has separated from the pack. Open-weight models read as more efficient, secure, and customizable. APAC-native models read as more sovereign for regulated industries — keeping data clear of US reach under regimes like the Cloud Act. The takeaway: OpenAI must win on three different trust axes at once, against a field that is still wide open.
02
Speed matters — there is no leader yet
No AI model vendor owns APAC today — the field is genuinely open. Competitors haven't formally set up at scale yet, and open-weight is fragmented and under-invested. But that won't last: vendors will start to lock up countries, markets, workloads, personas, and partners. Make these markets now, rather than fighting the uphill battle of the late entrant.
03
APAC buyers are different
More price-sensitive, more sovereignty-conscious, increasingly decentralized in decision-making, and far more dependent on vendor trust than US buyers. With recent trust concerns around OpenAI, cycles will run longer. The trust work needs to begin now — not when the deal is on the table.
04
APAC is not one market
It demands localized execution across several key markets. Local referenceability is core for both business and IT personas — they want stories for their use case, their industry, their country, and several of them. Not just case studies: narratives, speakers, data, advocacy. Dark marketing rules — senior buyers trust their circle of peers, not vendors or analysts.
05
Value over adoption
Enterprises everywhere are shifting from driving adoption to driving value from it — sharper still in price-sensitive APAC. Expect gradual, outcome-based utilization and expansion. Delivering value and outcomes from deployments is the game, and a strong partner ecosystem is what makes it happen at scale.
The "so what" — four strategic imperatives
01

Make markets, move first

Land priority verticals and personas before competitors formalize their APAC presence.

02

Lead with trust

Trust, security, and sovereignty as the foundational narrative — starting now, not at deal time.

03

Win on local peer proof

Advocacy and in-market, in-vertical reference at the center of the plan, not the periphery.

04

Sell outcomes, deliver via partners

Outcomes are the proof APAC buyers need before they buy; partners deliver value at scale.

Two motions, in parallel

Creation and capture are not sequential handoffs. Both run simultaneously; the weight shifts as buyers move through awareness. Create is heaviest when buyers are not in market; Capture grows as they approach a decision.

The concentration thesis

Do fewer things perfectly. Disproportionate energy goes to four bets; everything else is foundational — maintained and reliable, but not where we over-invest. The bets are the heroes; the engines and foundation execute them.
