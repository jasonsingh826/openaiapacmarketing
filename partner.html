Partner Marketing · The Engine Within

As models commoditize, the ecosystem becomes the moat.

Partner marketing ensures OpenAI's ecosystem is optimized for onboarding, enabled to build and go to market independently, and supported for growth acceleration. This is the plan to make OpenAI the platform APAC's partners build on — across five partner types, four partner audiences, and twelve markets.

Part 1 · What partner marketing is

Partners power every stage of the buyer journey.

Partner marketing sits at the intersection of three functions: it works with the Partnerships team (who own the commercial relationship), with regional and country marketing leads (who own in-market execution), and with global marketing (who own the program architecture). The partner marketing lead is the connective tissue — translating global programs into regional execution, and feeding regional reality back into global planning.

In APAC, where enterprise trust is earned through local proof and local delivery, partners aren't a side function — they're the coverage model, the trust layer, and the value-realization engine. At every stage of the buyer journey, a partner is present:

1

Unaware

The partner carries the OpenAI message into markets, verticals, and conversations we can't reach directly. An SI recommending OpenAI to their client base opens doors no outbound campaign can.

2

Problem aware

The partner validates the category and the need through their own client relationships. When a GSI tells a board "you need an AI platform strategy," that carries more weight than any vendor saying the same thing.

3

Solution aware

The partner recommends OpenAI by name — one of the most powerful compelling events in the entire journey. A trusted SI saying "we've built our practice on OpenAI" moves a buyer from evaluating to validating faster than any campaign.

4

Brand solution aware

The partner commits to implementation and ongoing delivery — the final trust proof. A buyer in APAC doesn't just buy the model; they buy confidence that someone local can deploy it, support it, and realize value from it.

5

Post-close

The partner drives value realization, produces outcomes, and feeds the advocacy engine. The partner who delivers the outcome and then talks about it publicly is the single highest-leverage marketing motion in the entire B2B plan.

The three focus areas

Disproportionate energy goes here. Everything else is foundational.

Focus 01

Communications & enablement

All four partner audiences — Partner Sales Leaders, Solutions Architects, Marketing Managers, and Alliance Managers — properly enabled to independently build, sell, and go to market with OpenAI.

Focus 02

Joint activation, top 5–10

High-touch co-investment with strategic partners — co-built campaigns, joint events, shared pipeline targets, dedicated resources — that drives real joint demand.

Focus 03

Seamless recruitment & onboarding

The front door is effortless and targeted — the right partner types in the right markets, activated to first value fast enough that they don't stall.

Part 2 · The strategic case

Why partner marketing is the highest-leverage investment in APAC.

The commoditization thesis

As frontier models converge on capability, partners stop choosing a platform because the model is better on a benchmark. They choose because it's easier to join, easier to build competency on, and more profitable to grow with. Partner marketing owns that value exchange. In a commoditizing market, the ecosystem is the moat — the model is table stakes.

The APAC amplifier

Everything that makes APAC harder for direct enterprise sales makes partners more valuable. Trust gates — partners are the trust shortcut. Sovereignty concerns — partners who've cleared compliance gates for other platforms can clear them for OpenAI. Decentralized buying — partners sit inside five-persona committees through existing relationships. Local referenceability — partners provide the in-country, in-vertical proof that APAC buyers demand. Price sensitivity — partners deliver the outcomes that justify the spend.

Without an ecosystem, OpenAI is a US vendor selling cold into trust-gated markets. With one, it has local proof, local delivery, and local credibility in twelve markets simultaneously.

The competitive urgency

Anthropic moved first. The Claude Partner Network launched March 2026, backed by $100M — 40,000+ applicants, 10,000+ certified consultants before OpenAI's Partner Network went live.

OpenAI is responding at scale. The Partner Network launched June 2026 with Accenture, Bain, BCG, McKinsey, PwC. Target: 300,000 certified consultants by year-end. But global launch ≠ APAC activation — that's this role's job.

Open-weight is the quiet threat. Sovereignty and customization appeal to APAC's regulatory instincts. The value exchange has to overcome the sovereignty argument.

The choice thesis — why a partner would choose OpenAI

A partner evaluating OpenAI is weighing three alternatives. The strategy answers all three:

vs. open-weight

Managed, trusted, improving

  • No DIY burden, no infrastructure overhead
  • TCO + trust + continuous improvement
  • Faster time-to-market for partner solutions
  • Lower delivery risk
vs. Anthropic

Breadth, platform, pull

  • 900M+ users creating bottoms-up demand
  • Fuller platform: Frontier, Workspace Agents, full API
  • Larger, more formalized partner network
  • Frontier Alliance GSIs already activated
vs. going it alone

Co-invest, co-sell, co-grow

  • MDF, dedicated resources, joint campaigns
  • Pipeline they can't generate alone
  • Certification that wins enterprise deals
  • A brand that opens doors theirs doesn't
Part 3 · The ecosystem

Who the partners are and how they're structured.

Five partner types, five different value exchanges

Each type plays a different role, enters the maturity cycle at a different point, moves at a different pace, and needs different programs.

Trust + delivery

SIs & consulting

GSIs and local SIs who architect, deliver, and manage enterprise deployments. Open doors, carry trust, deliver at scale. Slowest to activate, highest pipeline value. Frontier Alliance partners (Accenture, BCG, McKinsey, Capgemini) are the APAC starting roster; local SIs per market are the next wave.

Innovation + breadth

ISVs & builders

Software companies and startups building on the platform. Drive ecosystem breadth, platform lock-in, and innovation. Fast to activate — weeks, not months. Value compounds as their products create reasons to stay on the platform.

Coverage + transaction

Resellers

Package, price, and transact — especially mid-market and regional. Provide market coverage and transaction velocity. Fastest to activate, most volume-dependent.

Reach + creative

Agencies

Creative, media, and digital agencies building on the platform for clients. Extend reach and bring creative credibility. Top-of-funnel partners who generate awareness through their own client work.

Product surface

Technology partners

Snowflake, Databricks, Salesforce — platforms whose products integrate with OpenAI. Extend the product surface and create stickier solutions. An enterprise already on Snowflake + OpenAI is harder to displace.

Part 3 · The four partner audiences

A partner is not one person — it's four audiences.

Getting this wrong is the most common failure mode in partner marketing: programs designed for "the partner" as if it's one human being. Each function inside a partner needs a different enablement track, different content, different proof, and a different credential.

Audience 01

The Partner Sales Leader

The people who position and close deals involving OpenAI. They need to win deals — which means sales plays, demo scripts, ROI tools, competitive positioning, and deal-desk access.

What they care about: "Will OpenAI help me close bigger deals, faster?"

Credential: Sales Accreditation → unlocks co-sell, lead flow, deal registration

Audience 02

The Partner Solutions Architect

The people who build and integrate solutions on the platform. They need dev docs, build labs, reference architectures, sandboxes, integration guides, and technical office hours.

What they care about: "Is this platform reliable, well-documented, and does it give me the tools to build?"

Credential: Technical Certification → unlocks listing, co-build, priority support

Audience 03

The Partner Marketing Manager

The people who generate demand through the partner's own channels. They need campaign-in-a-box, co-marketing kits, brand guidelines, event templates, and digital content kits.

What they care about: "Does OpenAI make it easy for me to run campaigns that generate leads?"

Credential: Marketing Certification → unlocks MDF eligibility, co-marketing support

Audience 04

The Partner Alliance Manager

The people who run the partnership day-to-day. They need JBP templates, MDF process, QBR frameworks, operating cadence, and ongoing program communications.

What they care about: "Is the program well-organized, and does my effort translate into tier advancement?"

Credential: Program Accreditation → unlocks tier advancement, strategic access

Part 3 · Tiering and certification

Three tiers — earned, not bought.

Certification is the gate. It ensures partners who receive MDF and co-sell access can actually deliver — protecting customer experience and OpenAI's brand.

Tier 01

Registered

Free entry, self-serve. Portal access, foundational content, starter enablement, sandbox. No MDF, no co-sell, no listing. The front door — barrier to entry is zero.

Tier 02

Certified

One or more audience certifications complete. Unlocks: MDF eligibility, co-sell status, deal registration, marketplace listing, lead flow, full comms cadence, enablement events. The working tier.

Tier 03

Strategic

Multi-certification + pipeline + customer proof + exec sponsorship. Unlocks: dedicated resources (people, not just funding), joint planning with QBR rhythm, executive access, priority roadmap briefings, Tier-1 event co-presence. The top 5–10.

Part 3 · Best-in-class benchmarks

What the best programs in the world do — and what we borrow.

Studied five programs. The pattern is identical: open entry, role-based certification that gates real benefits, tiered co-investment, a co-sell engine, outcome-based measurement. Each contributed a specific mechanic:

AWS

Certification unlocks money

Competency validation is a top-3 buyer selection criterion. 65% of partners close faster and 54% win larger deals via co-sell. Certification must unlock real pipeline — not just a badge.

Google Cloud

Reward outcomes, kill admin

Recognition shifted to pre-sales influence and post-sales success over paperwork. AI automates tier tracking. Partners spend time with customers, not on forms.

Snowflake

Role-based, fast, localized

Persona-based learning tracks reaching competency in weeks, free, with regional-language rollout. The model for APAC-scale enablement.

Databricks

Scale the trained base

Tripled the trained partner base in 12 months using scaled, role-based learning. Treat enablement as a growth engine, not a cost center.

Anthropic

Open door, structured start

Free-to-join with structured 60-day onboarding and a Partner Success Manager. Low-friction entry paired with guided activation.

The synthesis

The APAC edge: executing this spine localized, by market — faster and more in-market than anyone else.

Part 4 · The engine

Recruit, enable, scale — the partner maturity cycle.

Three stages, each flowing into the next. A partner who isn't onboarded can't be enabled; one who isn't enabled can't scale. Each stage is comprehensive: what we do, for which partner types and audiences, through which programs, and measured how.

1
Stage 01

Recruit — get the right partners in, make it effortless to start

The goal is not volume. It's getting the right partner types in the right markets, activated to first value fast enough that they don't stall. Recruitment is targeted and seamless: targeted so we get the ecosystem composition we want, seamless so partners don't drop off before they start.

Program

Targeted recruitment campaigns

Digital: always-on, segmented by partner type and market. Physical: presence at events where target partners gather. Different channels for different types — GSI executive outreach ≠ ISV developer event ≠ reseller channel campaign ≠ agency industry event.

Program

Partner value proposition

Published, per type: what you get at each tier (access, enablement, MDF, co-sell), what's expected in return. The document that answers the choice thesis concretely — a partner BD lead puts this in front of their leadership.

Program

Frictionless enrolment + guided activation

Self-serve sign-up, fast agreements, immediate portal access. Then a structured first-90-days path with milestone triggers: first build, first certification, first co-marketable win. Partner Success contact for strategic-tier; automated nurture for the long tail.

Program

Recruitment through proof

Existing partners recruiting the next partner through advocacy and visible success. The best recruitment campaign is a partner who's winning publicly. This connects Stage 3 back to Stage 1 — the flywheel.

Measured by

New partners recruited (by type, by market) · time-to-first-value · onboarding completion rate · % activated within 90 days

2
Stage 02

Enable — make them independently able to drive business

The single biggest lever — and the stage most programs do badly. A partner is four audiences, each needing a different track and a credential at the end. Certification is the connective tissue: it gates tiering, MDF, co-sell, which turns enablement into a growth flywheel rather than a cost center.

3
Stage 03

Scale — invest with dollars and people to accelerate growth

Enabled partners need acceleration. Two gears: programmatic scale (1:many) for the certified base, and high-touch co-investment (1:1) for the strategic top 5–10. MDF is the fuel; joint planning is the engine; shared pipeline is the outcome.

1:many · certified partners

Scaled programs

  • → MDF marketplace — funds with pipeline targets, gated by certification, simple apply/approve/report, ROI tracked quarterly
  • → Campaign-in-a-box — pre-built, partner-branded campaigns deployable with minimal customization
  • → Syndicated content — OpenAI content partners co-brand and distribute
  • → Partner-led webinars & events — templated, supported, speakers available
1:1 · strategic top 5–10

High-touch programs

  • → Joint demand campaigns — co-built, shared accounts, shared pipeline targets
  • → Joint events — co-hosted summits, exec roundtables, workshops
  • → Integration into company-led campaigns — partners on the stage, not in the audience
  • → Dedicated resources — marketing people allocated to the partner's plan
  • → Joint ABM — coordinated motions into shared target accounts
  • → Customer proof — joint case studies and co-presented references
The expansion flywheel

Partners driving value realization → outcomes create advocacy → advocacy creates proof → proof recruits the next partner → the cycle compounds. Stage 3 feeds Stage 1.

Measured by

Partner-sourced pipeline · partner-influenced pipeline · MDF utilization & ROI · co-marketed closed-won · joint campaign ROI · partner attach rate · partner-led expansion revenue

Part 5 · The operating model

How APAC partner marketing actually runs — three levels, one system.

Global sets the architecture. APAC translates, adapts, and drives. Country leads localize last-mile and execute in-market. The system only works if all three levels are connected, with clear ownership, decision rights, and a planning rhythm.

Global / Central (US)

Sets the architecture

  • → Program architecture: tiers, certification, benefits
  • → Global partner agreements & commercial terms
  • → MDF policy & approval thresholds
  • → Brand guidelines & global messaging
  • → Frontier Alliances & Partner Network governance
  • → Portal, CMS, enablement platform
  • → Measurement framework & attribution
APAC Partner Marketing (this role)

Translates, adapts, drives

  • → APAC partner strategy & annual plan
  • → Regional partner tiering priorities
  • → Localized enablement & certification
  • → Joint plans with top 5–10 APAC partners
  • → APAC partner events & comms cadence
  • → Regional measurement & reporting
  • → Representing APAC in global planning
Country Marketing Leads

The in-market execution engine

  • → In-market partner recruitment & activation
  • → Language & cultural localization
  • → Local partner events & moments
  • → Local customer proof & references
  • → In-country partner relationship support
  • → Local MDF execution & reporting
  • → Earliest signal on what's working
Why country leads matter

APAC is not one market. The Korean ecosystem operates differently from Singapore, which operates differently from India. A recruitment campaign that works in ANZ won't work in Korea. Certification delivered only in English won't activate Japanese SIs. Country leads are not optional — they are the localization engine that makes the APAC strategy credible in each market.

Partner marketing ↔ partnerships (commercial)

Partnerships owns the relationship, commercial terms, JBP, partner contract, and the "handshake" — they decide which partners to pursue.

Partner marketing owns the programs that make the relationship productive: enablement, comms, certification, demand gen, MDF, joint campaigns.

They co-plan (partnerships sets priorities; partner marketing designs programs) and co-own pipeline (partner-sourced and influenced pipeline is a shared metric). Weekly sync on partner health, campaigns, and escalations.

Planning rhythm

  • → Annual: APAC plan aligned with global. APAC feeds priorities into global planning. Country leads input local priorities.
  • → Quarterly: KPI review across all maturity stages. Plan adjustments. Country leads present market performance.
  • → Monthly: Enablement output, MDF utilization, campaign performance. Country leads report what's landing.
  • → Weekly: In-flight campaigns, events, partner issues. Short, tactical, action-oriented.

Part 7 · Measurement

A diagnostic system, not a dashboard.

Three levels. Every lagging metric traces backwards through the maturity cycle to a leading one you can act on. The measurement model mirrors the engine.

Level 1
Business outcomes — quarterly to leadership
Partner revenue · partner net-new accounts · partner attach rate
Recruit
Partners recruited · time-to-first-value · activation rate
Enable
Certification coverage · engagement score · content utilization · NPS
Scale
Partner pipeline · MDF utilization · co-marketed closed-won · attach rate
Level 3
Campaign diagnostics — per-program, reviewed weekly, not reported to leadership
The diagnostic logic

If partner revenue is flat → check partner-sourced pipeline (scale) → if low, check MDF utilization → if unused, check certification coverage (enable) → if low, check activation rate (recruit) → if low, check onboarding friction. Every problem traces back through the cycle to an actionable root cause.
